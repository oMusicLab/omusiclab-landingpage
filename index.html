musician tools

Small, focused utilities for everyday practice — follow the beat, get the tuning right, and transpose chords when the song isn’t in your key.

Practice tools

Metronome

Follow the beat — set a tempo, tap it in, and keep steady time while you practice.

Tuner

Get the tuning right — guitar, ukulele, and violin modes with a clear pitch readout from your mic.

Chord Transposer

Handy chord transposing — paste your chords and move them to the key you’re actually playing in.

Printable resources

Guitar Chords Chart

Blank fretboard diagrams on a printable sheet — a clean slate for sketching fingerings and studying chord shapes.

Ukulele Chords Chart

Empty ukulele chord grids ready to print — fill in diagrams by hand for practice, lessons, or your own reference.

Earlier releases

Legacy

Older projects we keep around for reference — still online, but not part of the current omusiclab lineup.

Piano chord viewer

See piano chords on a virtual keyboard — handy for checking spellings and inversions away from the fretboard.

Chord diagram viewer

See how fret numbers map to fingers and open strings — a visual-first path through guitar and ukulele diagrams without drowning in prose.

Basic tabs generator

Scratch out ideas quickly with a simple tabs surface: write, tweak, and share lightweight sheets before you dive into full notation.

Simple guitar & ukulele chord diagram finder

Look up guitar and ukulele chord diagrams quickly — a lightweight finder from the early omusiclab toolkit.

Simple guitar & ukulele tabs editor

Draft guitar and ukulele tabs in a simple editor — jot down fingerings and lines without a full notation suite.

Guitar & ukulele music scale generator

Explore scales on guitar and ukulele fingerboards — pick a root and pattern to see notes laid out on the fretboard.
